## Design Context

### Users
Enterprise teams and professionals creating demo decks. They need to produce polished presentations quickly for sales demos, client pitches, and internal reviews. They value speed, ease of use, and professional output.

### Brand Personality
Playful, creative, warm. The tool should feel approachable and inviting -- like a creative collaborator, not a cold productivity tool. Professional enough for enterprise, but with personality.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference**: Canva -- easy, intuitive, users can maneuver with minimal friction
- **Anti-reference**: Generic AI tools, existing slide builders that feel clinical
- **Theme**: Dark mode (warm, not pure black) with light mode support
- **Avoid**: Purple, any colors that feel unprofessional or "AI-generated"
- **Palette direction**: Warm teal primary, coral accent, warm neutral backgrounds (charcoal with warm tint, NOT pure black)

### Design Principles
1. **Effortless navigation** -- Every action should feel obvious. Reduce cognitive load. Progressive disclosure over overwhelming options.
2. **Warm professionalism** -- Enterprise-grade output wrapped in a friendly, creative interface. No cold, sterile dashboards.
3. **Distinctive, not decorative** -- Every visual choice should serve a purpose. No gradient text, no glassmorphism, no glow effects used as decoration.
4. **Rhythm over repetition** -- Varied layouts, asymmetric compositions, visual hierarchy through spacing and weight rather than identical card grids.
5. **Confidence through clarity** -- Clear typography hierarchy, intentional color usage, obvious interactive states.
